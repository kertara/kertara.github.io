Kertara Messenger (v0.1.0-alpha)

Description: A messaging application that focuses on privacy by hiding your identity using the Tor network.

Developers: Yonatan Kristian, Joshafat Abdiel Budhianto.

How Does It Work?

Think of Kertara Messenger like sending a letter through a secret courier. Instead of going through a main post office that keeps a record of who sent what to whom, this app creates a "secret path" directly between the sender and the receiver using the Tor network.

Key Features:

  • Hidden Network: All messages pass through the Tor network, which scrambles your message's travel path so that your location or internet address (IP) cannot be easily tracked by outsiders.
  • Double-Locked Messages: Every message you send is locked with a digital code. Only the intended recipient has the "key" to unlock and read it.
  • Local Control: All your conversation history is stored directly on your own computer, not on a company server. You are the only one in control of your data.

Risks and Limitations (Important!)

Because this is still an early version (alpha), the app is currently in the experimental stage. Please keep these points in mind:

  • Not Fully Tested: The security technology we built is still in its early stages and has not been reviewed by outside security experts, so there may be undiscovered flaws.
  • Traces on Your Computer: While we try our best to delete sensitive data, computer operating systems (like Windows) sometimes save small pieces of information in temporary memory. If someone else hacks your computer, they might be able to find those pieces.
  • Pattern Awareness: Even though the content of your message is safe, the time you send messages and how often you communicate could theoretically be seen if someone watches your internet connection for a very long time.
  • Not for High-Risk Communication: Please do not use this app for highly sensitive or high-risk matters at this time.
